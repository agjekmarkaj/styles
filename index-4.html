Biography of Thomas Hardy

Who is Thomas Hardy?

Author, Poet (1840-1928)

BOOKS POEMS

Thomas Hardy was an English novelist and poet who set his work--including The Return of the Native and Far from the Madding Crowd--in the semi-fictionalized country of Wessex.

Synopsis

Thomas Hardy was born in Dorset, England in 1840. As a novelist he is best known for his work set in the semi-fictionalized county of Wessex including Tess of the d'Urbervilles and
Jude the Obscure. He was also an accomplished poet. Hardy died in 1928. His ashes are in the Poet's Corner of Westmister Abbey and his heart is buried in Stinsford with his first wife.
